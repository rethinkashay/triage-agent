Burns — Triage Guidelines

EMERGENCY:
- Large burns > 5–10% body surface area.
- Burns on face, hands, groin, or airway.
- Electrical or chemical burns.
- Burns with difficulty breathing or soot around the mouth.

URGENT:
- Blistering burns.
- Severe pain not responding to simple measures.
- Signs of infection: redness, fever, pus.

ROUTINE:
- Minor small-area first-degree burns.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Cool water for 10–20 minutes.
- Do NOT apply ice directly or toothpaste.
- Cover with clean, non-stick dressing.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. Call emergency services for severe burns.
