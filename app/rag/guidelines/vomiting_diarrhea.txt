EMERGENCY:
- Signs of severe dehydration: no urine, dizziness, confusion.
- Vomiting blood or black stool.
- Severe abdominal pain with vomiting.
- Symptoms after suspected poisoning.

URGENT:
- Persistent vomiting for more than 12 hours.
- Diarrhea lasting more than 48 hours with fever.
- Bloody diarrhea.

ROUTINE:
- Mild food-related stomach upset.
- Occasional loose stools with no red flags.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Hydrate with ORS, avoid heavy foods.
- Seek care sooner if symptoms worsen.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call emergency services.
