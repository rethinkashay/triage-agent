Respiratory infections like COVID-19 and influenza can range from mild to severe.

Common mild-to-moderate symptoms:
- Fever, chills, muscle aches
- Cough, sore throat, runny nose
- Fatigue and headache
- Loss of taste or smell (in some cases)

Emergency warning signs include:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath at rest
- Chest pain or pressure
- Confusion or difficulty waking
- Bluish lips or face
- Persistently high fever with worsening condition

People at higher risk for severe illness include older adults, pregnant individuals, and those with chronic heart, lung, kidney, or immune conditions.

Mild cases often improve with rest, fluids, and fever control.
Follow local public health guidance regarding testing, masking, and isolation.
Seek medical advice if symptoms worsen or if there is any concern about breathing or oxygen levels.
