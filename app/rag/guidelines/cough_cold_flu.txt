Cough / Cold / Flu — Triage Guidelines

EMERGENCY:
- Severe shortness of breath or blue lips.
- Chest pain with breathing.
- Confusion, severe weakness, or inability to stay awake.
- High fever not responding to medication.

URGENT:
- Persistent fever > 38.5°C for more than 48 hours.
- Cough with wheezing or difficulty breathing.
- Productive cough with blood.

ROUTINE:
- Mild runny nose, sore throat, or cough.
- Symptoms improving with home care.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Rest, hydration, and warm fluids.
- Use paracetamol/ibuprofen for fever.
- Seek care if symptoms worsen or last beyond 7–10 days.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call local emergency services.
