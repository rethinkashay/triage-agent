EMERGENCY:
- Sudden hearing loss.
- Ear pain with facial weakness or drooping.

URGENT:
- Ear pain with fever.

- Pain with dizziness or vomiting.

ROUTINE:
- Mild discomfort after swimming or altitude changes.
- Ear fullness or popping sensations.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Warm compress may help reduce pain.
- Avoid inserting cotton swabs or objects into ear.
- If symptoms last more than 48 hours, seek care.
