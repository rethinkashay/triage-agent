EMERGENCY:
- Difficulty breathing, wheezing, or tightness in the throat.
- Swelling of lips, tongue, or face.
- Widespread hives with dizziness or fainting.
- Suspected anaphylaxis after food, medication, or insect sting.

URGENT:
- Hives spreading quickly without breathing problems.
- Facial or eyelid swelling without airway involvement.
- Reaction after a new medication without severe symptoms.

ROUTINE:
- Mild itching or localized skin rash.
- Mild hives that improve with antihistamines.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Avoid suspected allergen.
- Antihistamines can relieve mild symptoms.
- Seek urgent care if swelling or breathing issues develop.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call local emergency services.
