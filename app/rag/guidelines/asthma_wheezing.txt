EMERGENCY:
- Severe shortness of breath not relieved by inhaler.
- Unable to speak full sentences.
- Blue lips or fingernails.
- Peak flow < 50% of normal (if known).

URGENT:
- Wheezing not improving after 2 inhaler rounds.
- Persistent cough with shortness of breath.
- Night-time awakenings due to breathing difficulty.

ROUTINE:
- Mild wheeze that improves with inhaler.
- Occasional cough after exercise.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Use rescue inhaler as directed.
- Avoid smoke, dust, and allergens.
- Seek escalation if symptoms worsen or inhaler is not relieving.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call local emergency services.
