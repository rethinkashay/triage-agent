EMERGENCY:
- Sudden severe joint pain with fever and redness (possible septic joint).
- Joint injury with obvious deformity or inability to bear weight.

URGENT:
- Swelling with warmth and stiffness.
- Pain not improving for several days.
- Joint pain with rash or recent infection.

ROUTINE:
- Mild strain from overuse.
- Temporary stiffness after activity.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Rest, ice, elevation.
- Avoid overuse until pain improves.
- Gentle stretching once pain decreases.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. Seek emergency care for severe joint symptoms.
