EMERGENCY:
- Severe flank pain with fever and chills (possible kidney infection).
- Inability to urinate with severe lower abdominal pain.
- Blood in urine with large clots.
- Urinary symptoms with confusion (especially in elderly patients).

URGENT:
- Painful urination with fever.
- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine with back pain.
- Frequent urination with pelvic pain.
- Visible blood in urine (no clots).

ROUTINE:
- Mild burning on urination without fever.
- Frequent urge to pee but no pain or fever.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Drink plenty of fluids.
- Avoid caffeine and alcohol during symptoms.
- Seek care sooner if fever, back pain, or worsening symptoms develop.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies call local emergency services.
