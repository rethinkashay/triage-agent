Abdominal Pain — Triage Guidelines

EMERGENCY (seek immediate medical attention or call emergency services):
- Sudden severe abdominal pain that is unbearable.
- Pain with a hard, rigid abdomen.
- Pain with vomiting blood or blood in stool (black or bright red).
- Pain with chest pain, fainting, difficulty breathing, or severe dizziness.
- Severe abdominal pain in pregnancy with bleeding, dizziness, or fainting.

URGENT (see a doctor the same day):
- Pain worsening steadily over several hours.
- Pain with fever above 38.5°C.
- Pain with persistent vomiting or inability to keep fluids down.
- Pain with painful urination, flank pain, or blood in urine.

ROUTINE / SELF-CARE:
- Mild cramps or stomach discomfort improving with rest.
- Symptoms after overeating or mild indigestion.
- Mild constipation or gas without severe symptoms.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Sip fluids and avoid heavy foods.
- Avoid painkillers like ibuprofen if stomach pain is worsening.
- Seek care sooner if pain spreads, worsens, or new symptoms appear.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
These guidelines are for information only and do not replace professional medical evaluation.
For emergencies, call local emergency services.
