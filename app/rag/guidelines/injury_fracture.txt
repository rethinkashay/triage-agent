EMERGENCY:
- Visible bone deformity or open fracture.
- Inability to move or feel the limb.
- Severe bleeding that does not stop with pressure.
- Neck or spine injury after trauma.

URGENT:
- Painful swelling after a fall.
- Suspected minor fracture without deformity.
- Limited range of motion due to pain.

ROUTINE:
- Mild sprains with minimal swelling.
- Bruises improving with time.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE).
- Avoid putting weight on injured areas.
- Seek care if swelling or pain worsens.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies call local emergency services.
