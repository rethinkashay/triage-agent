EMERGENCY:
- Sudden dizziness with weakness on one side or facial droop.
- Chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations.
- Repeated fainting episodes.
- Dizziness after head injury.

URGENT:
- Dizziness with vomiting or dehydration.
- Lightheadedness when standing that persists.
- Fainting once with lingering symptoms.

ROUTINE:
- Mild dizziness from standing quickly.
- Brief episodes from skipped meals or dehydration.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Eat regularly, hydrate well.
- Rise slowly from sitting or lying.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. Call emergency services for severe symptoms.
