Child Fever — Triage Guidelines

EMERGENCY:
- Fever with difficulty breathing, blue lips, or severe lethargy.
- Fever with seizures.
- Child not waking or responding normally.
- Fever in infants under 3 months (>38°C).

URGENT:
- Fever lasting more than 48 hours.
- Fever with rash, stiff neck, or vomiting.
- Fever with dehydration: dry mouth, no tears, poor urine output.

ROUTINE:
- Fever responding to paracetamol/ibuprofen.
- Child active and drinking normally.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Encourage hydration.
- Use age-appropriate fever reducers.
- Monitor for worsening symptoms.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call emergency services.
