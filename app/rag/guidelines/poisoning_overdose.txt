EMERGENCY:
- Suspected overdose of medication, alcohol, or drugs.
- Ingestion of chemicals, cleaners, pesticides, or unknown substances.
- Difficulty breathing, confusion, seizures, or unconsciousness.
- Severe vomiting after ingestion of spoiled food or toxins.

URGENT:
- Mild abdominal pain or nausea after accidental ingestion.
- Exposure to fumes with persistent cough or eye irritation.

ROUTINE:
- Very small accidental ingestion with no symptoms (e.g., child tastes non-toxic item).

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by professionals.
- Rinse mouth with water after accidental ingestion.
- Contact poison control if available in your region.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. Poisoning is often an emergency. Call emergency services immediately.
