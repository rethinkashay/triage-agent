EMERGENCY:
- Rash with difficulty breathing, facial swelling, or throat tightness.
- Purple spots that do not blanch (possible meningococcal infection).
- Rapidly spreading rash with fever.

URGENT:
- Painful rash with blisters or pus.
- Rash with fever lasting more than 24 hours.
- Suspicion of shingles (burning pain + band-like rash).

ROUTINE:
- Mild itchy rash without fever.
- Red patches after new soap, lotion, or detergent.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Avoid scratching and use gentle skin care.
- Antihistamines may reduce itching.
- Seek urgent care if rash spreads quickly or new symptoms appear.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. Seek medical care for severe rashes.
