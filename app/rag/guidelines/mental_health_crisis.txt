EMERGENCY:
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide.
- Threats of harm to others.
- Severe confusion, hallucinations, or inability to care for oneself.
- Sudden behavioral changes with violence or agitation.

URGENT:
- Persistent severe anxiety or panic interfering with daily tasks.
- Depression symptoms worsening rapidly.
- Insomnia for several days with functional decline.

ROUTINE:
- Mild anxiety, low mood, or stress-related symptoms.
- Difficulty concentrating or emotional distress without safety concerns.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Talk to a trusted person and seek support.
- Practice grounding techniques for anxiety.
- Follow up with a mental health professional if symptoms persist.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. If there is any risk of harm to self or others, call emergency services immediately.
