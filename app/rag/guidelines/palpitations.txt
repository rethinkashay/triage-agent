EMERGENCY:
- Palpitations with chest pain, fainting, or severe shortness of breath.
- Heart racing above 150 bpm at rest.
- Irregular heartbeat following drug use or stimulant ingestion.

URGENT:
- Palpitations lasting more than 30 minutes.
- Palpitations with dizziness or near-fainting.
- New irregular heartbeat without severe symptoms.

ROUTINE:
- Brief episodes lasting seconds and resolving spontaneously.
- Palpitations after caffeine, anxiety, or stress.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Avoid caffeine, nicotine, and stimulants.
- Monitor heart rate when possible.
- Seek urgent care if symptoms worsen or persist.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call local emergency services immediately.
