EMERGENCY:
- Sudden loss of bladder or bowel control.
- Severe weakness or numbness in both legs.
- Back pain with chest pain, fainting, or abdominal tearing sensation.
- Back pain after high-impact trauma (accident, fall).

URGENT:
- Pain with leg numbness or tingling.
- Severe pain not improving with rest.
- Back pain with fever or unexplained weight loss.

ROUTINE:
- Muscle strain from lifting.
- Mild stiffness improving with movement.

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Heat/ice, gentle stretching, short-term rest.
- Avoid heavy lifting until symptoms improve.

SAFETY DISCLAIMER:
Not medical advice. For emergencies, call local emergency services.
